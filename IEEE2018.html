IEEE Awareness Session 2018

By: Gobiga Rajalingam


The 1st step we took together as IEEE Student Branch of RUSL was conducted on the 28th of November 2018 as the IEEE Awareness Session 2018. A few weeks following the Annual General Meeting to appoint the first Committee of IEEE Student Branch of RUSL, the event was organized in order to introduce IEEE, the largest professional association to the youth of RUSL.

The event commenced at 10.30 am at the Auditorium of the Faculty of Applied Sciences, with the arrival of the Guest Speakers Mr.Peshan Sampath, the IEEE SAC Chair, Miss.Nuwini Dulanga, the Sectional Student Representative at IEEE Sri Lanka Section and Mr. Chanaka Hettige, the Chair of IEEE Innovation Nation Sri Lanka 2018. Following the Registration process of around 150 participants and the lighting of traditional oil lamp, the sessions began. During the whole period of the event, participants were given with the introduction about IEEE volunteering, IEEE membership and Sri Lanka section and the significance of IEEE. With a small gaming activity by Miss. Gobiga Rajalingam to cheer up the crowd, the event started off as a lively one and proceeded off to the sessions.

The 1st session was on “Volunteering with IEEE” by Mr. Peshan Sampath which comprised of the benefits of volunteering, an introduction to IEEE and his experiences in IEEE. It was a highly informative session, which handed out motivation to the audience. The next session of the day was done by Miss. Nuwini Dulanga, the SSR 2018. The interactive session was on “IEEE Membership with Sri Lanka Section”, which majorly included the activities done by IEEE Region 10, Sri Lanka Section and the Student Branches,volunteering opportunities and her life in IEEE. Following that, we had a gaming activity via Kahoot and swags were distributed to the top winners. The final speech was delivered by Mr.Chanaka Hettige, on “IEEE, a Lifestyle” explaining the opportunities and responsibilities that come along in IEEE if we are willing to start volunteering somewhere.

The whole event was winded up with a “Vote of Thanks” by the Secretary of the branch, Miss. Pavithra Nanayakkara. To be succinct, the amount of enthusiasm and contribution we received on the day from the Guest speakers to the University Administrative staff, was admirable and it undeniably has created an unforgettable memory in the minds of everyone in RUSL.
